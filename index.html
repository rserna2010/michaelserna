Who I Am

I received my degree from the University of Southern California in 2012 where I studied Finance and Economics. During my career, I've had the opportunity to work at the US. Department of Treasury with PERAB and on Wall Street as a summer analyst with Citi in their investment banking group. I help build and run a Los Angeles mayoral campaign with a team of two young college grads. We started out of the candidate’s living room and turned it to a $700K operation with over 30 full-time staff and 175 volunteers. Did we win? No. Moral victory? Yes.

I then taught Algebra in East Los Angeles with Teach for America where I was confused for a student at times, raised test scores, but far more important to me, helped my kids understand we were each other’s brothers keeper and how bettering our community was dependent on paying it forward.

So why technology? My passion for tech began on Wall Street where I worked in the Technology, Media, and Telecom investment banking group with Citi. I did analysis for tech companies on their IPO, potential Merger & Acquisition targets, and valuation models. However, I realized quickly I wanted to be closer to building the technology that was changing the way we lived, and not simply sitting on the sidelines watching other people create.

I taught myself the core concepts of web development before I decided to go all in and enrolled at MakerSquare (Hack Reactor Core School), an immersive software engineer program, where I built a number of applications as a full stack engineer. I am now currently working for Finix Payments, a white-labeled payments platform for payment facilitators and integrated software vendors, as a contract software engineer.
